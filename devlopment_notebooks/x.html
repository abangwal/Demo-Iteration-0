competition analysis

Nike
strength
  • Strong brand awareness with a high brand value of $53.7 billion
  • Huge customer base with millions of customers worldwide
  • America's favorite sneaker brand
  • Aimed sustainability
  • Iconic relationships with famous athletes like Michael Jordan
  • Ability to maintain and enhance side brands like Converse and Hurley
  • Low manufacturing cost as majority of footwear is manufactured in foreign countries
  • In-house professionals who design shoes and athletic accessories
  • Superior marketing capabilities
  • Black community support through ad campaigns addressing racism
weakness
  • Poor labor conditions in foreign countries including forced labor, child labor, low wages
  • Retailers' stronger hold making it weak due to sensitivity to pricing
  • Pending debts with total long-term debt of $8.9 billion
  • Lawsuits related to labor practices and intellectual property
  • Lack of diversification over-reliance on sporting footwear and apparel
  • Contradicting strategies for shifting facilities to renewable energy
  • Dependency on North America for sales revenue
opportunities
  • Expansion in emerging markets like India, China, Brazil
  • Continuous innovation for new products combining technology with athletic wear
  • Efficient integration through acquisitions or partnerships with manufacturers
  • Reduction of dependence on big retailers focusing on direct-to-consumer sales
  • Acquisition of small or medium-sized companies for product offerings expansion
  • Merges with Metaverse companies for virtual shoes creation
threats
  • Counterfeit products damaging reputation and revenue
  • Increased competition from new emerging brands like Hoka and New Balance
  • Marketing budget pressure from competitors like Adidas and Lululemon increasing their marketing budgets
  • Currency foreign exchange risks due to global operations vulnerability
  • Patent disputes with competitors like Adidas
  • Economic uncertainty affecting sales revenue
  • Trade tensions impacting global supply chain revenue
timeline
  • 1964: Nike is founded by Bill Bowerman and Phil Knight as Blue Ribbon Sports
  • 1971: Blue Ribbon Sports becomes Nike, Inc., named after the Greek goddess of victory
  • 1972: Nike releases its first running shoe with the iconic swoosh logo
  • 1980s: Nike introduces several innovative shoe technologies such as Air cushioning system
  • 1990s: Nike continues to innovate with Shox technology and launches NikeiD, allowing customers to customize their shoes
  • 2000s: Nike focuses on sustainability by reducing waste in manufacturing processes
  • 2013: Nike introduces Boost technology through a partnership with Adidas (this is not entirely accurate - Boost technology is actually owned by BASF and was first used by Adidas in 2013, but Nike has developed similar technologies like React foam and ZoomX since then))
  • 2017: Nike launches ReactX, a new midsole material that reduces carbon footprint by at least 43%
  • 2018: Nike invests in emerging trends like bioprinting and AI-powered design
  • 2021: Nike reports significant growth in footwear sales and continues to expand its distribution channels including e-commerce and direct-to-consumer sales
descriptionNike is an American multinational corporation that designs, develops, and sells athletic footwear, apparel, and equipment. It was founded in 1964 by Bill Bowerman and Phil Knight under the name Blue Ribbon Sports, initially operating as a distributor for Onitsuka Tiger before launching its own brand, Nike, in 1971. Nike is known for its iconic swoosh logo and its wide range of products including running shoes, basketball shoes, football cleats, soccer cleats, and apparel such as t-shirts and hoodies. The company offers popular product lines like Air Jordan in collaboration with Michael Jordan and lifestyle shoes like Air Force 1 and Presto. Nike's products are available in over 190 countries worldwide through various retail channels including Nike-owned stores, specialty running stores, and online retailers.

Adidas
strength
  • Strong brand value that customers enjoy associating with
  • Innovative product development and research
  • Diversified product portfolio that caters to various sports and leisure markets
  • Strategic partnerships with well-known sports organizations, athletes, and celebrities
  • Global presence with business conducted in 160 countries
  • Strong digital presence through online storefronts and social media engagement
weakness
  • Dependence on a complex global supply chain that can result in disruptions
  • Smaller product line compared to competitors
  • Rising operating expenses including labor, marketing/advertising costs
  • Heavy dependence on the cyclical sports market
  • Outsourced manufacturing that exposes the company to risks related to working conditions
  • Dependence on physical retail stores during pandemic times
opportunities
  • Growing e-commerce trend that can be leveraged through partnerships with e-commerce platforms
  • Increasing demand for gender-neutral clothing/shoes
  • Growing sportswear industry that presents an opportunity for increased sales
  • Premium products demand in developing countries
  • Use of smart materials that can enhance comfort/functionality of sports gear
  • Expansion into new markets such as wellness/athleisure wear
threats
  • Intense competition from established brands like Nike/Puma/Under Armour
  • Risk of supplier dominance due to reliance on vast network of suppliers
  • Presence of counterfeit goods that impact reputation/financial results
  • Economic downturns that decrease consumer spending on luxuries like sports gear/shoes
  • Lawsuits related to unlicensed technology usage (Flyknit) that can result in financial losses/reputation damage
  • Potential negative impact from geopolitical events such as trade wars or pandemics.
timeline
  • 2023 Q1: Adidas reports an 8% growth in sales, with revenue growing to €5.46 billion and operating profit reaching €336 million.
  • Adidas' footwear sales rise by 13% in constant currency terms to €3.24 billion, driven by lifestyle products such as Originals.
  • Apparel sales increase by 2% to €1.88 billion, while income from accessories falls slightly by 1% to €328 million.
  • Adidas makes €150 million from selling leftover Yeezy stock and still has around €200 million worth of inventory left.
  • CEO Bjorn Gulden emphasizes that the brand is strong without Yeezy and that core business is growing at a rate of 5% ahead of plans.
  • Most regions see growth except for North America where sales fall by 3.6% due to high inventory levels and low consumer demand.
  • Adidas focuses on localization and collaborations with local actors to drive growth in specific regions.
  • Adidas expects currency-neutral revenues to increase by a mid-to-high single-digit rate in the current year.
  • Operating profit expected to reach around €700 million.
  • Adidas continues shedding existing Yeezy stock and expects to sell remaining inventory over remainder of year at average cost.
  • CEO Bjorn Gulden expresses frustration over end of partnership with Germany's national football team starting in 2027.
  • Earnings and income taxes rise by €554 million compared to same period last year.
descriptionAdidas is a German multinational corporation founded by Adolf 'Adi' Dassler in 1949. It specializes in designing and manufacturing shoes, clothing, and accessories. The company is known for its innovative designs, focus on sustainability, and high-profile endorsement deals with athletes like Lionel Messi and celebrities like Kanye West. Adidas generates most of its revenue from footwear (55%) and sportswear and fashion-oriented apparel (38%).
offerings
  • Athletic shoes: Adidas offers a wide range of athletic shoes for various sports such as running, soccer, basketball, tennis, golf, etc. Prices vary depending on the style and technology used but typically range from $50 to $200 or more for high-end models like Yeezy Boosts or Ultra Boosts
  • Apparel: Adidas provides a variety of sportswear for men, women, and children including t-shirts, shorts, pants, jackets, sports bras, etc. Prices range from around $20 for basic items to over $100 for premium pieces
  • Accessories: Adidas also sells bags, hats, socks, watches, sunglasses, fitness trackers & other accessories to complement their clothing & footwear offerings.
